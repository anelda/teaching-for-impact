# Template Style & Design Specification (v1)

This specification defines the required structure, design principles, and formatting rules for all activity templates in the workbook.

---

## 1. Core Structure (MANDATORY ORDER)

All templates MUST follow this exact structure:

## Template Title

## Version

## Purpose

## You will use

## Instructions

## Steps (numbered)

## Translation to your learners

## Context check

## Iteration

## Reuse in later sections

## Reflection

---

## 2. Design Principles

### 2.1 Templates are for the primary audience

Templates are designed for:
- Educated professionals
- First-time instructional designers

Requirements:
- Maintain clarity and structure
- Use precise, directive language
- Support independent completion

Do NOT:
- oversimplify
- remove necessary structure
- assume low literacy for the template user

---

### 2.2 Templates must force translation to the secondary audience

Every template MUST include:

## Translation to your learners

Purpose:
- Ensure training is adapted to real-world contexts
- Surface assumptions
- Force consideration of constraints

---

### 2.3 Templates must produce artefacts, not thoughts

Templates must result in usable outputs.

Requirements:
- Include clearly defined fields
- Use structured formats (tables, lists, labelled inputs)
- Ensure outputs can be reviewed or reused

Avoid:
- open-ended reflection without structure
- vague prompts such as "write your thoughts"

---

### 2.4 Templates must support iteration

Every template MUST include:

## Iteration

- v0 = draft
- v1 = revised
- v2 = final

Include:
- a prompt asking what will be improved next

---

### 2.5 Templates must be connected (system, not standalone)

Every template MUST include:

## You will use
- Reference at least one previous template

## Reuse in later sections
- Reference at least one future template

Purpose:
- Ensure continuity
- Enable cumulative design work
- Prevent one-off activities

---

### 2.6 Templates must enforce decision-making

Each template should include:
- at least one decision point
- at least one constraint or trade-off consideration

---

## 2.7 Dependency Enforcement (NEW)

Templates must REQUIRE the use of outputs from previous templates.

Requirements:
- "## You will use" must reference specific prior templates
- At least one step must explicitly require:
  - an output from a previous template
  - (e.g. outcome, learner constraint, system element)

Do NOT:
- make dependencies optional
- use vague references

Purpose:
- ensure templates function as a connected system
- prevent isolated completion of templates

---

## 3. Instruction Design Rules

### 3.1 Use directive, minimal language

Use:
- clear, action-oriented instructions

Examples:
- "Define 2–3 indicators"
- "List key constraints"

Avoid:
- vague phrasing
- explanatory or conceptual language

---

### 3.2 Use step-based progression

Structure all tasks as:

Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3

Avoid:
- long paragraphs
- unstructured instructions

---

### 3.3 Separate concepts from application

- Concepts belong in the workbook content
- Templates are for application only

Avoid:
- re-explaining theory inside templates

---

## 4. Output Design Rules

Each template must:

Include:
- clearly labelled fields
- structured outputs (tables, bullet points, defined sections)

Avoid:
- large blank areas
- ambiguous or undefined outputs

---

## 5. Context Integration Rules

Templates must explicitly include:

## Translation to your learners
## Context check

Purpose:
- ensure adaptation to real-world conditions
- surface constraints and risks

Do NOT:
- simplify templates for low literacy
Do:
- require the designer to adapt outputs for their learners

---

## 6. Reuse & Flow Rules

Each template must:

- reference at least one previous template
- reference at least one future template

Expected flow:

System Map → Learners → Outcomes → Activities → Evaluation → OER

---

## 7. Reflection Design Rules

Reflection must be:

- minimal (1–2 prompts only)
- focused on improvement and next steps

Use:
- "What will you improve next?"
- "What changed from your initial design?"

Avoid:
- long reflective journaling
- abstract questions

---

## 8. Versioning Standard

All templates must include:

## Version

- v0 = draft
- v1 = revised
- v2 = final

---

## 9. Formatting Rules

- Use consistent heading levels (##)
- Use bullet points for inputs and lists
- Use tables where appropriate
- Maintain consistent spacing and layout
- Ensure visual consistency across all templates

---

## 10. Quality Checklist (Before Finalising a Template)

Each template should meet the following criteria:

- Can be completed without a facilitator
- Produces a clear, usable output
- References previous and next steps
- Includes translation to learners
- Includes context constraints
- Supports iteration (v0 → v1 → v2)
- Uses clear, directive instructions
- Avoids unnecessary conceptual explanation
