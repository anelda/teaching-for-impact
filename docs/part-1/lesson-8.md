## Why this matters

Training is often evaluated based on:

- attendance  
- satisfaction (“Did participants enjoy it?”)  

But these do not tell you:

- whether learning happened  
- whether anything changed  
- whether the training had impact  

Effective training requires:

- **Assessment**: Understanding what learners have learned  
- **Evaluation**: Understanding whether the training made a difference  

!!! quote "▶️ This section helps you move from..."

    *“Did the training run?”*  

    to:

    *“Did learning happen, and did it lead to meaningful change?”*

---

## Assessment vs evaluation

---

### Assessment (learning-focused)

Assessment looks at:

- what learners know or can do  

Examples:

- completing a task  
- demonstrating a skill  
- explaining a concept  

---

### Evaluation (programme-focused)

Evaluation looks at:

- whether the training achieved its goals  

Examples:

- changes in behaviour  
- application in real contexts  
- longer-term impact  

---

### Why this matters

You need both:

- assessment to support learning  
- evaluation to understand impact  

---

## Formative vs summative assessment

---

### Formative assessment (for learning)

- happens during learning  
- provides feedback  
- supports improvement  

Examples:

- practice activities  
- peer feedback  
- discussion  

---

### Summative assessment (of learning)

- happens at the end  
- evaluates achievement  

Examples:

- final task  
- completed project  

---

!!! tip "Key takeaway"
    Prioritise formative assessment to support learning.

---

## Designing assessment aligned with outcomes

Assessment should reflect what learners are expected to do.

---

### Alignment question

> If learners complete this assessment, does it demonstrate the outcome?

---

### Example

**Outcome:**  
Use a tool to analyse data  

**Aligned assessment:**  
Analyse a dataset using the tool  

**Misaligned assessment:**  
Answer multiple-choice questions about the tool  

---

!!! warning "Common mistake"
    Assessing knowledge when outcomes require practical skills.

---

## Indicators of success

!!! abstract "Indicator"
    A clear, observable sign that learning or change has occurred.

---

Indicators help you measure:

- whether learning occurred  
- whether outcomes were achieved  

---

### Characteristics of good indicators

- specific  
- observable  
- relevant  
- realistic  

---

### Keep it simple

Focus on:

- 2–3 meaningful indicators  

Avoid:

- overly complex measurement systems

## Collecting evidence

You can gather evidence through:

- observation (what learners do)  
- outputs (what they produce)  
- feedback (what they report)  
- follow-up (what changes over time)  

---

### Practical considerations

- use simple methods  
- avoid creating unnecessary burden  
- focus on what is useful  

---

## Signal vs noise

Not all data is useful.

---
!!! abstract "Signal"
    Meaningful information that reflects real learning or change.

!!! abstract "Noise"
    Information that is irrelevant or misleading - does not reflect learning or impact.

---

### Example

Signal:

- participants successfully apply a method  

Noise:

- high attendance rates  

---

## Evaluation in real-world contexts

In many contexts:

- resources are limited  
- long-term tracking is difficult  

---

### ⚙️ Practical approach

- focus on immediate and short-term outcomes  
- collect simple, actionable data  
- prioritise usefulness over completeness  

---

## Feedback as part of evaluation

Feedback is not only for learners—it is also for you.

---

### Use feedback to:

- improve your training  
- identify gaps  
- adapt future sessions  

---

!!! tip "Key takeaway"
    Evaluation is not just about reporting—it is about improving.

---
## Ethical considerations

When collecting data:

- respect participant privacy  
- be transparent about purpose  
- avoid extractive practices  

---

## What this means in practice

When designing your training, ask:

- How will I know if learning happened?  
- What evidence will I collect?  
- What indicators matter most?  
- How will I use this information?  

---

!!! warning "Common mistake"
    Collecting data without using it to improve training.

---

## Common mistakes

- Measuring only attendance or satisfaction  
- Using assessments that do not align with outcomes  
- Collecting too much data without using it  
- Ignoring feedback from participants  
- Treating evaluation as an afterthought
