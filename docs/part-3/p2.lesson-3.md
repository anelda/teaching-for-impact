## Why this matters

In the previous sections, you identified resources and checked whether you are allowed to use them.

This section focuses on the next step:

> How do you make these resources work for your learners and your context?

Most resources are not designed for your specific setting.

They may:

- use unfamiliar examples  
- assume access to tools or infrastructure  
- reflect different cultural or institutional contexts  

If you use them as they are:

- learners may struggle to relate  
- activities may not work as intended  
- learning may not transfer into practice :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}  

!!! quote "This section helps you move from..."
    *“This resource is good—I’ll use it as it is.”*  
    to:  
    *“How do I change this so it works for my learners and context?”*

## Core concepts

### Adapt, localise, remix

!!! abstract "Adapt, localise, remix"
    Three complementary practices for making resources usable in your context: changing content or structure, adjusting context, and combining materials.

- **Adaptation** — change content or structure to support outcomes  
- **Localisation** — adjust context, examples, and language  
- **Remixing** — combine multiple resources into one experience  

Design implication: effective training often combines all three.

---

### Coherence

!!! abstract "Coherence"
    Ensuring that adapted or combined materials form a clear, consistent learning experience.

Coherent materials:

- follow a clear sequence  
- use consistent language  
- align with outcomes  

Design implication: integration matters as much as selection.

---

## Practical guidance

### Step 1 — Start with your outcomes

Before making changes, ask:

- What do learners need to be able to do?  

Use this to decide:

- what to keep  
- what to change  
- what to remove  

---

### Step 2 — Prioritise what to adapt

Focus your effort where it matters most:

- what affects understanding  
- what affects whether the activity works  

You do not need to change everything.

---

### Step 3 — Adapt for clarity and usability

Make materials easier to use:

- simplify or clarify instructions  
- adjust structure or sequence  
- modify level of difficulty  

Focus on making the activity work in practice.

---

### Step 4 — Localise for relevance

Make the material meaningful in your context:

- replace unfamiliar examples  
- adjust language and terminology  
- reflect local realities and constraints  

Ask:

> Would learners recognise this in their own context?

---

### Step 5 — Remix carefully

When combining materials:

- ensure they support the same outcome  
- align level and complexity  
- use consistent language and concepts  

Avoid combining materials without integrating them.

---

### Step 6 — Check coherence

After adapting or remixing:

- review the full activity or sequence  
- ensure instructions are clear  
- check that everything aligns with outcomes  

Ask:

- Could someone else use this without your explanation?  

---

### Step 7 — Document your changes

Keep track of:

- what you changed  
- why you changed it  

This supports future reuse and adaptation.

---

### Decision point

> What choice does the reader need to make here?

> How much effort will you invest in adapting and refining materials?

- Minimal adaptation (use mostly as-is)  
- Targeted adaptation (focus on key changes)  
- Intentional redesign (adapt, localise, and remix fully)  

Your choice affects relevance, usability, and learning outcomes.

---

## Example

**Context:**  
A team selects an activity on data analysis from an open resource.

**Decision:**  
Should they use it as-is or adapt it?

**Action:**  
They:

- simplify instructions  
- replace examples with local data  
- adjust the level of complexity  
- combine it with another activity  

They then review the full sequence for clarity and alignment.

**Outcome:**  
The activity is easier to understand, relevant to learners, and works effectively in practice.
