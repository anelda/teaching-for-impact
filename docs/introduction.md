# Introduction

---

## Why this workbook exists

Many researchers, practitioners, and professionals are asked to design and deliver training.

They often:

- have deep expertise in their domain  
- care about impact and real-world change  
- want to support others effectively  

...but have had little or no formal preparation in:

- how people learn  
- how to design effective learning experiences  
- how to create materials that can be reused and shared  

As a result, training often becomes:

- content-heavy rather than learning-focused  
- disconnected from real-world application  
- difficult to reuse or scale  

👉 This workbook is designed to help you move from:

- *“How do I deliver my knowledge?”*  
to:

- *“How do I design learning that leads to real change—and can be reused and shared?”*

---

## What this workbook will help you do

By working through this material, you will learn to:

- design training that leads to **real-world outcomes**, not just participation  
- create learning experiences that are **active, relevant, and effective**  
- work **with learners and communities**, not just for them  
- make intentional decisions about **reuse, adaptation, and creation**  
- develop materials that are **clear, usable, and shareable**  

---

## Training as part of a bigger system

Training is not just an event.

It is an **intervention in a system**:

- shaped by people, resources, and constraints  
- influenced by power, context, and access  
- dependent on whether learning can be applied in practice  

This means:

- good content alone is not enough  
- learning must connect to real contexts  
- design decisions must reflect what is actually possible  

---

!!! tip "Key takeaway"
    Effective training is not about delivering content—it is about enabling meaningful change in real-world contexts.

---

## The design workflow (how everything fits together)

This workbook is structured around a practical design workflow.

You can think of it as a series of connected decisions:

---

### 1. Understand the system and your learners  

- What context are you working in?  
- Who are your learners, and what shapes their participation?  
- What constraints and opportunities exist?

---

### 2. Define outcomes and impact  

- What should learners be able to **do** after the training?  
- What change are you trying to contribute to?  

---

### 3. Apply principles of how people learn  

- How do people move from exposure to understanding and application?  
- How can you support retention, practice, and transfer?

---

### 4. Design learning experiences  

- What will learners actually **do** to achieve the outcomes?  
- How will activities support participation and engagement?

---

### 5. Build in practice, feedback, and iteration  

- Where will learners practice key skills?  
- How will they receive feedback and improve?

---

### 6. Assess learning and evaluate impact  

- How will you know if learning has occurred?  
- What evidence will show meaningful change?

---

### 7. Design for reuse, adaptation, and sharing  

- Can this training be used again—by you or others?  
- Should you reuse, adapt, or create materials?  
- How will you make your materials usable beyond your presence?

---

!!! tip "Key takeaway"

    Each step builds on the previous one. Skipping steps often leads to ineffective or unusable training.

---

## An important shift: from content to learning

A common starting point is:
> “What content should I include?”

This workbook encourages a different approach:

> “What should learners be able to do—and how will I support that?”

This shift means:

- focusing on outcomes, not coverage  
- designing activities, not just presentations  
- supporting practice, not just explanation  

---

## Another shift: from one-time delivery to reuse

Many training programmes are designed for a single delivery.

But in practice, you will likely:

- run the training again  
- adapt it for new audiences  
- build on it over time  

Designing with reuse in mind helps you:

- save time  
- improve quality  
- extend impact  

👉 You are designing not only for your current participants, but also for:

- your future self  
- colleagues  
- other contexts  

---

!!! tip "Key takeaway"
    
    Training has greater impact when it can be reused, adapted, and shared.

---

## How to use this workbook

This workbook is designed to support both:

### During the programme

- follow along with sessions  
- complete activities and templates  
- reflect on your own training design  

### After the programme

- revisit sections as needed  
- refine and improve your materials  
- adapt your training for new contexts  

---

### Practical guidance

You do not need to apply everything at once.

Focus on:

- what is most relevant to your context  
- what will make the biggest difference  
- what is feasible within your constraints  

---

!!! tip "Key takeaway"

    Progress is more important than perfection. Small, thoughtful improvements can significantly strengthen your training.
