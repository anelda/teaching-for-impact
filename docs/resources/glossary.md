# Glossary

## Active Learning
Learning by doing—solving problems, discussing, or applying knowledge—rather than only listening or reading.

## Adaptation
Changing a resource (content, structure, or level) so it works for your learners and context.

## Assessment
Ways of checking what learners can do or understand during or after training.

## Attribution
Giving credit to the original creator of a resource, usually including author, title, source, and license.

## Co-Design
Designing training with participants or stakeholders, not just for them.

## Cognitive Load
The amount of mental effort required to process information. Too much leads to confusion or disengagement.

## Constructive Alignment
Ensuring that outcomes, activities, and assessment all support the same goal.

## Feedback
Information that helps learners understand what they are doing well and how to improve.

## Indicator
A clear, observable sign that learning or change has happened.

## Iteration
Improving something through repeated cycles of testing, feedback, and revision.

## License
A set of rules that defines how others can use, adapt, and share your work.

## Localisation
Adapting a resource so it fits a specific cultural, linguistic, or contextual setting.

## Mental Models
The existing understanding or assumptions that learners use to interpret new information.

## Needs Assessment
A simple process of understanding what learners need, already know, and what constraints they face.

## OER (Open Educational Resources)
Teaching and learning materials that can be reused, adapted, and shared because they have open licenses.

## Outcomes
What learners are able to do after the training.

## Outputs
Immediate results of training (e.g. completed tasks), not necessarily lasting change.

## Positionality
How your background and role influence how you design and deliver training.

## Retrieval Practice
Strengthening learning by recalling and applying knowledge, not just reviewing it.

## Reuse
Using an existing resource without changing it.

## Remixing
Combining parts of multiple resources into a new, coherent whole.

## Scaffolding
Providing support early in learning and gradually removing it as learners gain independence.

## Theory of Change
A simple explanation of how your training is expected to lead to outcomes and impact.

## Usability
How easy it is for someone to understand and use your materials without help.

## Versioning
Keeping track of changes to materials over time (e.g. v1, v2).

## ZPD (Zone of Proximal Development)
The space where learners can succeed with support but not yet independently.
